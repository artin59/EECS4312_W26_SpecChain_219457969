# Application: [Calm] 
- Most important difference between pipelines: The automated pipeline had really broad personas that didn't follow the VARIED principle. It also classified a lot of irrelevent reviews into the same groups. As for the requirements they were good requriements at their core, however they were not even close to being in good requirements format. In contrast to that the manual pipeline focused on a smaller subset of highly relevant reviews, which resulted in more precise and VARIED personas. Furthermore, the hybrid pipeline had the best of both worlds, however it did take the longest out of the 3 pipelines since it took a long time to fix the mistakes of the automatic pipeline.
- Most useful pipeline: In terms of content the hybrid pipeline was the most useful one, because it combined automation with manual refinement. However it did take quite a long time, mirroring almost the same time invested into the manual pipeline.
- Most surprising finding: The automated pipeline was not the best at grouping relevant reviews together. Even after a lot of refinement into the python script, it still ended up with weakly or not related review grouping which took a long time to fix in the hybrid pipeline.
- Observed weakness in the automated pipeline: Even though the automated pipeline had really high review coverage, it often classified irrelevant reviews into groups and generated personas with unsupported assumptions. This revealed that coverage alone isn't a reliable indicator of quality. Additionally, many of the generated requirements were not in good requirement format and were ambigous, making them difficult to validate through testing.  

Overall, despite taking more time than expected, the hybrid pipeline produced the most reliable and useful results by combining the strengths of both manual and automated approaches
